# Action: Race-day preparation

Use this action in the final 2–3 weeks before the goal race, or when the athlete asks specifically about race-day strategy, taper, fuelling, pacing, kit, or what to do the day before.

## Taper structure (last 2 weeks for a half marathon)

**Week -2 (14–8 days out):**
- Volume drops ~30% from peak week
- Intensity maintained — one short tempo or HM-pace block stays in the plan
- Long run ~12–14km, with the last 3–4km at HM pace as a final dress rehearsal
- Gym continues but loads drop ~30%

**Week -1 (race week):**
- Volume drops ~50% from peak
- Mon: rest or short shakeout
- Tue: short run with 4×30s strides at 5K pace
- Wed: full rest or 30-min walk
- Thu: 20-min easy with 2–3 strides
- Fri: 15–20 min easy or rest
- Sat (day before): full rest, gentle walk, hydrate well
- Sun: race

No new shoes. No new fuel. No new clothes. No new anything.

## Pre-race fuelling protocol

**3 days out:** start emphasising carbs. Not gluttonous — just heavier toward pasta, rice, oats, fruit. Hydrate consistently.

**Day before:** normal portions of familiar foods. Light protein, plenty of carbs, salt to taste. Avoid: heavy fibre, dairy if she's even slightly sensitive, alcohol, novel cuisines. Last big meal early evening, not late.

**Morning of:** 2.5–3 hours before start. Something she's eaten before plenty of runs. Toast + jam + banana is a classic. Coffee if she normally has coffee. 500ml water with electrolytes, finished 90 min before start.

**Last 30 min before start:** small carb top-up — half a banana, a gel with water, a few dates.

**During race (90+ min target):**
- One gel at 30 min in
- One gel at 60 min in
- Sip water at every aid station (not gulps — small sips)
- If hot day, a pinch of salt or electrolyte drink at half-way

## Pacing strategy

For Oxford Half target 1:40:00 (4:44/km pace):

- **Km 1–3:** 4:48–4:52/km. Deliberately slower than goal pace. Lots of energy in the legs, easy to go out hot. Don't.
- **Km 4–15:** 4:42–4:46/km. Settle into goal pace, hold.
- **Km 16–18:** 4:40–4:44/km. If she has more, this is where to find it.
- **Km 19–21:** whatever she's got. Empty the tank.

Cumulative: even or slight negative split. Crossed the line breathing hard. No reserves left.

If she goes out at 4:30/km in the first 3km because adrenaline, she will pay for it from km 15 onwards. Brief her in advance and review the first split data after the race.

## Race morning logistics checklist

- Race kit laid out night before — top, shorts, socks, sports bra, shoes
- Bib pinned to top
- GPS watch charged, set to display the metrics she wants visible
- Fuel pre-organised in pockets or belt
- Anti-chafe on relevant areas
- Toilet stop before bag drop
- Warm-up: 10 min easy jog + 3–4 strides about 20 min before start
- Cap or visor if sunny; light layers if cold (discard at start)

## Mental cues for race day

- **At the start:** "I trained for this. Let it come to me." First 3km feels easy. That's correct.
- **At halfway:** "I've done 10km in training many times. The race only starts now."
- **At km 17:** when it hurts and she wants to back off — "I'm at 4:42 pace. This is the pace I trained at. Hold."
- **Last km:** "Everything I've got, nothing left at the line."

## After the race

- Walk for 10 min minimum before sitting down
- Water + something with carbs + protein within 30 min
- Stretch / light mobility within the day if possible
- 7–10 days of complete rest from running (walks, yoga, pilates fine)
- Block 5 / next-block planning conversation about a week post-race
